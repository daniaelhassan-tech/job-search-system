## Acme Corp

- **What:** Enterprise SaaS platform. Mid-market vertical expansion — building industry-specific editions of their horizontal product.
- **Funding:** Public (ACME, ~$20B market cap). Stock down ~30% from highs due to sector rotation.
- **Revenue/scale:** ~$5B total revenue. Mid-market segment ~$1B, growing 40% YoY. ~1% TAM penetration in target verticals.
- **Team:** SVP Product (Maria Chen, ~4 months in, hired from competitor). Functional org: eng → CTO, GTM → CRO. Product is heavy PM — PMs expected to be "GMs."
- **Offices:** Mountain View HQ, NYC (growing tech hub), Austin, London.
- **Role:** Director of Product, Vertical Strategy. Newly created. Owns manufacturing, healthcare, tech verticals. Team partially built — PLs underneath for each vertical. Reports to Maria (SVP).
- **Comp estimate:** Director PM median TC ~$650K (range $500-900K per Levels.fyi). RSU math depends on stock recovery.
- **Key contacts:** Maria Chen (SVP Product, hiring mgr), Chris Yang (recruiter), Stacey Liu (EA).
- **Strategic context:** Mid-market is CEO's #1 strategic bet. First vertical edition (construction) launched in beta. This role builds the playbook for everything else. Company just announced AI partnership for industry agents.
- **Open questions:** Specific level/title? What does "manufacturing" mean concretely (job shops? discrete? process?)? Timeline for first non-construction vertical? How much autonomy on vertical prioritization? How does this interface with the AI agent strategy?
