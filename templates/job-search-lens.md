## Viability Threshold

<!-- This is the first-pass gate. Run this BEFORE evaluating whether the opportunity is exciting. -->
<!-- If it doesn't clear the threshold, the evaluation lenses don't matter. -->

**The central question: If I leave or this doesn't work out in 18-24 months, am I better positioned than I am today?**

This is what "stability" actually means. Not runway. Not whether the company survives. Whether the experience, brand, skills, network, and narrative I accumulate make my next move stronger.

Concretely, the threshold has [N] parts (customize — here are common ones):

1. **Positioning value:** Does this role give me a story, credential, or skill set that makes me more valuable on the market in 18-24 months? A brand-name company preserves optionality. A seed-stage company that folds requires a clear accomplishment to point to. An obscure company with a vague title is the worst case.

2. **Benefits + personal constraints:** [Customize: health insurance requirements, family planning timeline, visa constraints, whatever is non-negotiable for your life right now. State it plainly — these aren't aspirations, they're hard constraints.]

3. **Comp floor: $[X]K TC.** This is the minimum to say yes. Non-negotiable. Everything above this is about trajectory, not viability.

4. **Location:** [Your location constraints. Be specific: "NYC-based or NYC-friendly with real office presence" is different from "remote-ok" is different from "anywhere but must be in EDT timezone."]

5. **Commitment horizon:** Can I see myself here for at least [N] years? Not "will the company last" but "is this interesting and stable enough that I'd want to stay?" If the answer requires hoping things work out, it's not stable enough.

**How to use this:** If an opportunity clears all parts, evaluate it on the lenses above. If it fails any one, it needs an extraordinary compensating factor to stay in the pipeline — and you should name that factor explicitly rather than handwaving.

**Paths that get there:**
<!-- Customize for your level and market. Examples: -->
- Big tech [Level]+ ($[X]K base + RSUs)
- Growth-stage company approaching IPO (equity vests into real value)
- Early-stage with meaningful equity % AND platform economics AND a credible $1B+ outcome

**Paths that probably don't:**
- Seed-stage services companies with low-multiple valuations
- Public companies with flat stock and no upward trajectory
- Roles where equity is token or illiquid

**The key question for each opportunity:** What's the comp story in year 1, year 3, year 5? If the answer for year 5 requires 3+ things to go right that are outside my control, the financial trajectory is speculative.

## Tensions I'm Actively Managing

<!-- These are the tradeoffs you're navigating. Name them so your AI can reference them when they come up. -->

1. **Innovation vs. positioning safety.** The most transformative roles are at the least proven companies. Evaluating case by case, but the viability threshold is the floor.
2. **Domain depth vs. domain lock.** [Your domain] expertise opens doors but may limit how people see me. Every [domain] role reinforces the pattern. A non-[domain] role could break it.

4. **Comp trajectory vs. learning opportunity.** Willing to take a comp hit for the right positioning, but the floor is the floor.
