## Acme Corp

### Call: Maria Chen (SVP Product) — [DATE]
**Source:** Recruiter screen with Chris Yang led to hiring manager intro. 45 min video call.

**Key signals:**
- Maria is designing this role in real-time — no fixed JD yet. "We need someone who can figure out what manufacturing means for us."
- Emphasized builder culture: "Not putting hundreds of people on this. Crack eggs, iterate."
- Confirmed NYC is a "growth site" for product and engineering. Was positive about location.
- Did not share specific comp range — call was a sell/culture pitch, not a screening.
- Stock context came up. Acknowledged SaaS selloff but positioned it as "market misunderstanding."

**What I think:**
- This is bigger and more interesting than expected. Building the vertical playbook for industries the company doesn't yet know is a strategy + discovery challenge, not just execution.
- The "GM, straw that stirs the drink" framing matches what I want — ownership of outcomes, not just delivery.
- Manufacturing at 80% focus is a question mark. I don't have deep manufacturing domain experience. Need to confirm with Maria: does she want domain expertise or vertical product strategy expertise? Big difference.
- Maria's sell was heavy. Lots of culture, mission, opportunity framing. Less on specifics. Typical for senior recruiter screens. The substance comes in the next conversation.

**Open threads:**
- [ ] Hiring manager interview [DATE] — the real conversation
- [ ] JD incoming — review when received
- [ ] What does "manufacturing" mean concretely in mid-market?
- [ ] Comp range — need to surface in next round

## Helios AI

### Call: Sam Okafor (CEO) — [DATE]
**Source:** 30 min intro call. Routed by two separate talent partners at [VC firm] — strong external signal.

**Key signals:**
- Sam designing role in real-time: "Just after talking to you, that'd be kind of like a role." No predefined req.
- Double-routing from VC = external validation of fit.
- Explicitly framed as services business, not software. "We're not selling the software."
- Gave homework: "take a look at [acquired company]" — expects observations on transformation opportunity. This is a test.
- Passed on a $1.2B acquisition target — shows discipline, but also means platform isn't ready for scale.

**What I think:**
- Operating Partner track is the interesting one. Closest thing to founding available in the pipeline. Product Lead under CTO risks being scoped as senior PM.
- Sam is a legit domain visionary (3 exits, 20+ yrs). Not a first-time founder learning on my time.
- Financial concern: seed stage, PE-style valuations. Comp gap vs. big tech is real ($150-250K/yr in year 1). Need to understand equity structure before this is serious.
- The AI depth question: thesis is AI-centric but business is services-first. Daily work may be more operational than I want.

**Open threads:**
- [ ] Research [acquired company] (CEO homework — HIGH priority)
- [ ] Follow-up email with observations (48hr window)
- [ ] Equity structure clarity needed before serious evaluation
- [ ] Need to understand platform build status vs. thesis
